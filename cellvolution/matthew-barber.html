Matt Barber

 

Post-doc

evolutionary genetics & cell biology

Matt grew up in Rockville, Maryland before moving to Japan.  Returning to the U.S. as an undergrad at Colgate University, he joined the lab of Ken Belanger to study the nucleocytoplasmic transport of transcription factors in budding yeast.

 

After receiving his B.A. in Molecular Biology, Matt moved to California to pursue a Ph.D. at Stanford University.  He trained in the labs of Katrin Chua and Or Gozani, where his thesis work focused on characterizing the biochemical and cellular functions of mammalian sirtuin proteins.

 

In the Elde lab, Matt is combining his molecular biology experience with evolutionary approaches to study the implications for molecular arms races in host-pathogen interactions.

 

In his free time Matt enjoys cooking, traveling, and a variety of sports.
