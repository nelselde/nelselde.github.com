Diane grew up in Pennsylvania Amish country and moved west to attend college. After obtaining a B.S. in Biology from BYU, she fell in love with the mountains and red-rock of Utah and decided to make Salt Lake City her permanent home.

 

Diane has worked as a technician at the U of U for many years, starting in Keith Crutcher's lab studying the role of growth factors in neuronal plasticity and mechanisms of neuronal degeneration. Diane used electron microscopy techniques to study sprouted sympathetic fibers in the hippocampal formation of the rat brain, as well as the activation-dependent redistribution of talin in platelets after joining the lab of Mary Beckerle. While in the Beckerle lab, Diane became interested in using cellular and molecular biology to answer basic questions and has been pursuing those interests ever since. 

 

Diane worked in the Wayne Border & Nancy Noble lab investigating the pathogenesis of transforming growth factor beta and extracelluar matrix deposition in glomerulonehpritis and diabetic nephropathy. More recently, Diane worked as an HHMI associate in the lab of Alejandro Sanchez studying the molecular basis of regeneration in the freshwater planarian Schmidtea mediterranea.

 

Diane is a member of the 'slow-but-still-happy-to-be-here' triathlon-cycling-running community. She lives with her husband, two daughters, and an ever changing assortment of animals. Diane and her family love snowshoeing, hiking and camping in the rocky mountain region, volunteering at Best Friends Animal Sanctuary, cooking adventures, and listening to all kinds of music.
