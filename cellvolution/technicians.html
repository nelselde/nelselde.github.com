Job Summary

The Elde Lab studies host-pathogen evolution using model systems to investigate genetic conflicts and understand how fundamental cellular processes are influenced by pathogens. Our research is multi-disciplinary and draws on a variety of approaches from molecular evolution, genetics, virology, cell biology, and functional genomics. Projects include the acquisition of gene datasets from diverse primates and viruses, phylogenetic analysis, deep-genome sequencing, and functional testing of hypotheses (e.g., yeast genetic assays, viral infections in tissue culture- including experimental evolution).


Responsibilities

This Laboratory Technician position will involve data acquisition and analysis (extensive database queries, DNA/RNA preparation, RT-PCR, DNA sequencing, yeast genetics, and mammalian tissue culture). Previous experience using general techniques of molecular biology, virology, and/or molecular evolutionary analysis is highly desirable. Additional experience with Unix, Perl, and related computational tools is also useful. In addition to research, responsibilities associated with this position include assisting with daily lab operations (ordering, maintaining stocks, and general lab organization), and presenting data at lab meetings and other venues.


Qualifications

Bachelor's degree in Biology or related scientific discipline or equivalency required; knowledge of complex laboratory techniques, equipment, terminology, materials and substances. Skilled in the use of laboratory equipment, ability to create and analyze statistical calculations and prepare reports required; and demonstrated human relations and effective communication skills also required.


How To Apply

Please send a cover letter, CV, and list of three references with contact information to Nels Elde.
